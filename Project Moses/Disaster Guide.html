Typhoon

A powerful tropical cyclone that generates extreme sustained winds, heavy rainfall, and dangerous storm surges. It poses a severe threat by causing widespread flooding, landslides, uprooted trees, and structural damage to buildings, frequently resulting in major power and communication outages.

Before

  • Secure doors and windows
  • Prepare food, water, and medicine
  • Charge power banks and phones

What to Expect

  • Rapidly rising floodwaters.
  • Severe damage to roofs and infrastructure.
  • Prolonged power and water outages.

What to Bring

  • Battery-powered radio and flashlight.
  • Whistle for signaling.
  • Minimum three days of non-perishable food.

During

  • Stay indoors and away from windows
  • Do not go outside during strong winds
  • Listen to official announcements

After

  • Check for damages and leaks
  • Avoid floodwater and fallen wires
  • Help others if safe

Earthquake

A sudden, violent shaking of the ground, caused by the movement of tectonic plates. Earthquakes can cause severe ground rupture, liquefaction, landslides, and trigger devastating fires and tsunamis. Immediate hazards include falling debris, shattered glass, and structural collapse of buildings.

Before

  • Secure heavy furniture
  • Know safe spots in your area
  • Prepare emergency supplies

What to Expect

  • Violent, rapid ground shaking.
  • Frequent, unpredictable aftershocks.
  • Gas leaks and broken utility lines.

What to Bring

  • First aid kit and essential medications.
  • Sturdy shoes and protective gear.
  • Water for 72 hours (3 days).

During

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold
  • Move away from glass and shelves
  • Stay calm and protect your head

After

  • Check for injuries
  • Turn off gas if leaking
  • Expect aftershocks

Flood

An inundation of normally dry land caused by persistent heavy rain, overflowing rivers, or drainage system failure. Floods are extremely dangerous due to fast currents, contaminated waters, and hidden debris, leading to the destruction of property, disruption of transport, and risk of waterborne diseases.

Before

  • Move valuables to higher places
  • Prepare emergency kit
  • Know evacuation routes

What to Expect

  • Swiftly rising, opaque water levels.
  • Road closures and impassable areas.
  • Loss of communication services.

What to Bring

  • Waterproof bag for documents.
  • Clean drinking water and purification tablets.
  • Rubber boots and rain gear.

During

  • Go to higher ground
  • Avoid walking in floodwater
  • Listen to authorities

After

  • Disinfect your surroundings
  • Check for structural damage
  • Boil drinking water

Landslide

The swift downward movement of rock, soil, or debris under the influence of gravity, often triggered by intense rainfall or seismic activity. Landslides are highly destructive, burying anything in their path, including roads, homes, and critical infrastructure, and can occur with little to no warning in susceptible areas.

Before

  • Avoid staying near steep slopes
  • Plant trees on bare soil
  • Observe warning signs

What to Expect

  • Sudden, fast-moving flows of mud and debris.
  • Blocked roads and severed utility lines.
  • Loud rumbling or grinding sounds.

What to Bring

  • Personal protective equipment (gloves, helmet).
  • Rope or strong cord.
  • Emergency food and water.

During

  • Move away immediately
  • Run to higher ground
  • Protect your head

After

  • Do not return immediately
  • Watch for secondary slides
  • Report damaged areas

Fire

An uncontrolled combustion that threatens life and property, particularly dangerous in dense urban areas where it can spread rapidly between structures. Urban fires are characterized by extreme heat, heavy smoke, and potential explosion risks from gas or stored chemicals, necessitating rapid evacuation and professional firefighting response.

Before

  • Check electrical wiring
  • Have fire extinguisher ready
  • Avoid overloading outlets

What to Expect

  • Thick, toxic smoke and heat.
  • Rapid structural damage and collapse risk.
  • Emergency service response and evacuation orders.

What to Bring

  • Smoke masks or damp cloths.
  • Small fire extinguisher (if trained to use).
  • Keys and essential documents.

During

  • Stay low to avoid smoke
  • Use exits, not elevators
  • Call emergency hotline

After

  • Do not re-enter the building
  • Seek medical help if needed
  • Wait for clearance

Tsunami

A series of powerful, large waves generated by an underwater disturbance, typically an earthquake, volcano, or landslide. Tsunami waves travel across the ocean at high speed and strike coastal regions with immense force, causing widespread flooding, erosion, and devastation that can extend far inland.

Before

  • Move to higher ground quickly
  • Know warning signs
  • Prepare emergency kit

What to Expect

  • A sudden, significant recession of the ocean (drawdown).
  • A rapid succession of massive waves.
  • Strong currents carrying heavy debris.

What to Bring

  • Life jacket or flotation device.
  • Sturdy hiking boots.
  • Map of local high ground/evacuation routes.

During

  • Stay away from the coast
  • Follow evacuation routes
  • Wait for all-clear

After

  • Avoid flooded areas
  • Beware of debris
  • Help injured people

Drought

An extended period of unusually dry weather that persists long enough to cause severe ecological and economic damage, including water supply shortages, crop damage, and reduced power generation. Drought conditions significantly heighten the risk of severe wildfires and negatively impact public health and food security.

Before

  • Store clean water
  • Fix leaking pipes
  • Use water wisely

What to Expect

  • Strict water rationing and usage limits.
  • Increased risk of wild and urban fires.
  • Strain on crop yield and food prices.

What to Bring

  • Extra water storage containers (jerrycans).
  • Water-saving fixtures and tools.
  • Moisturizing cream and lip balm.

During

  • Limit water use
  • Avoid wasting water
  • Follow water schedules

After

  • Clean containers
  • Continue conserving
  • Report dry areas

Volcanic Eruption

The venting of hot material‚Äîmagma, ash, gas, and rock fragments‚Äîfrom a volcano's vent. Eruptions pose multiple hazards, including fast-moving pyroclastic flows, deadly mudflows (lahars), and thick ashfall that can contaminate water, damage aircraft, and cause breathing problems.

Before

  • Know evacuation zones
  • Wear mask and goggles
  • Prepare emergency supplies

What to Expect

  • Heavy ashfall causing low visibility.
  • Acid rain and sulfurous gas odors.
  • Possible lahars (volcanic mudflows) in valleys.

What to Bring

  • P3-rated respirator masks (or N95).
  • Goggles or protective eyewear.
  • Plastic sheeting and duct tape for sealing doors/windows.

During

  • Stay indoors
  • Avoid river channels
  • Follow official orders

After

  • Remove ash carefully
  • Check roof stability
  • Wait for clearance
